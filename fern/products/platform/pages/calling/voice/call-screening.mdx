To protect customers, carriers, and end users from fraud, SignalWire runs every outbound and inbound call through an automated screening layer. The layer is account-aware: brand-new accounts are held to tighter limits, and the limits relax as the account establishes a calling history.

This page covers when screening activates, what is evaluated, and how to keep legitimate traffic flowing.

## When screening activates

Screening starts on an account's **first call** — inbound or outbound — not on the date the SignalWire Space was created.

This means a Space that was created weeks or months ago, but has never placed or received a call, is treated as brand-new the moment it makes its first call attempt. From that first call forward, the screening window is measured against that timestamp.

<Info>
This is a recent change. Previously, screening was measured from the date the Space was created, which let dormant Spaces age past the new-account screening window without ever placing a call. Anchoring on the first call closes that gap.
</Info>

## What screening evaluates

The screening layer combines several signals. None of them are exposed as configurable knobs to customers — they are tuned by SignalWire's fraud team — but understanding the categories helps explain why a call may be blocked.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Account maturity" icon="regular hourglass">
    How long it has been since the account's first call. Newer accounts have lower per-hour and total call limits than mature accounts.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Destination type" icon="regular phone-arrow-right">
    Toll-free, SIP, and PSTN destinations are evaluated independently. Toll-free destinations are the most restricted for new accounts.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Origin patterns" icon="regular signs-post">
    Calls placed from toll-free numbers, and SIP-to-PSTN traffic, have additional limits during the early-account window.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Number reputation" icon="regular shield-exclamation">
    Calls from or to numbers on SignalWire's internal blocklist are rejected outright, regardless of account age.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

A call that exceeds these limits is marked as spam and rejected. SignalWire's fraud team is also alerted so the activity can be reviewed.

## What this looks like to your application

When a call is blocked by screening, the call attempt fails before connecting to the destination. You will see this in the call's status and in the SignalWire Dashboard the same way you would see any other rejected call.

If your application makes calls programmatically, handle these rejections the same way you handle other call failures — surface the error and back off rather than retrying immediately, since rapid retries themselves contribute to the patterns that trigger screening.

## Avoiding false positives on new accounts

A few things help legitimate traffic pass the new-account window cleanly:

- **Warm up gradually.** Build up call volume over the first several days rather than placing the maximum volume on day one.
- **Use the right destination types.** Calls to standard PSTN destinations have looser limits than calls to or from toll-free numbers.
- **Avoid placing calls from a Space and then leaving it idle for long stretches.** A long gap in activity may be re-evaluated as a fresh start.
- **Get verified for high-volume use cases.** If you have a legitimate use case that needs higher throughput from day one, [contact Support](mailto:support@signalwire.com) so the account can be reviewed and allow-listed.
