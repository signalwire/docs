To protect customers, carriers, and end users from fraud, SignalWire runs every outbound and inbound call attempt through an automated screening layer. The layer enforces volume-based limits during a new account's first 21 days of calling activity, with tighter limits for the first 2–3 days.

This page documents when screening starts, the exact limits, and what to do if you hit them.

## When screening starts

Screening is anchored on the account's **first call** — inbound or outbound — not the date the SignalWire Space was created. A Space that has never placed or received a call is treated as brand-new the moment it makes its first call attempt, regardless of how long ago the Space was created.

<Info>
This is a recent change. Previously, screening was measured from the Space creation date, which let dormant Spaces age past the new-account screening window without ever placing a call. Anchoring on the first call closes that gap. Accounts older than one year that have no recorded calling activity are treated as mature so legitimate dormant accounts are not rate-limited when they finally start calling.
</Info>

Once the account has been actively calling for **21 days**, screening no longer applies and call volume is governed only by [Space-level rate limits](/docs/platform/rate-limits).

## Per-hour limits during the first 21 days

For the first 21 days of calling activity, each call is checked against a per-hour limit that depends on the destination type and the age of the calling history:

| Scenario                                          | Calls per hour allowed |
| ------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| From a toll-free number, first 3 days             | 2                      |
| To a toll-free number, first 3 days               | 2                      |
| To a SIP destination, first 3 days                | 10                     |
| All other destinations (PSTN long code)           | 25                     |

The per-hour limit is multiplied by the number of hours since the account's first call to give a running total budget. So a regular PSTN account that has been calling for 4 hours has a budget of `25 × 4 = 100` calls, and that budget grows by 25 every additional hour.

## Total-call limits in the first 2 days

Inside the first 2 days of calling activity, the per-hour formula above is overridden by a flat total limit:

| Scenario                                                                                     | Total calls allowed |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| Account is on the high-volume calling allow list                                             | 90,000              |
| Either the from-number or the to-number is toll-free                                         | 5                   |
| All other destinations                                                                       | 20                  |

After day 2, the per-hour formula takes over.

## SIP-to-PSTN special case

Calls originating from a SIP endpoint (`@` in the from-number) and terminating on a PSTN number have a stricter cap of **25 total calls** for the entire 21-day window. SIP-to-PSTN traffic is one of the most-abused patterns for toll fraud, so the limit deliberately does not scale with time inside the window.

## Number blocklist

A call is rejected regardless of account age if the from-number appears on SignalWire's internal blocklist of known fraudulent numbers. The blocklist is maintained by the fraud team — customers cannot add or remove entries directly.

## Allow-listing for legitimate high-volume use

For customers with a verified high-volume calling use case (for example, an outbound dialer or contact center spinning up a new Space), there are two ways to bypass screening:

- **High-volume calling allow list.** Projects on this list get the 90,000-call ceiling described above for their first 2 days, and the regular per-hour formula afterward.
- **Bypass flag on the Company.** When `bypass_musubi_call_limits` is set on the Company, screening is skipped entirely for the account's calls.

Both are configured by SignalWire — contact [Support](mailto:support@signalwire.com) if your use case needs one of them.

## What happens when a call is blocked

When a call attempt exceeds a limit:

1. The call is rejected before connecting to the destination.
2. The call's status reflects the rejection the same way any other failed call does.
3. SignalWire's fraud team is alerted (once per company per hour) so the activity can be reviewed.

If your application makes calls programmatically, handle these rejections the same way you handle other call failures — surface the error and back off, since rapid retries themselves push you further past the limit.

## Avoiding false positives on new accounts

A few things help legitimate traffic pass the new-account window cleanly:

- **Warm up gradually.** Build up call volume over the first several days rather than placing the maximum volume on day one.
- **Watch the per-destination split.** A new account calling toll-free destinations is on the 2-calls-per-hour track during the first 3 days. Calls to standard PSTN destinations have a much higher 25-per-hour budget.
- **Don't put a SIP-to-PSTN dialer on a new Space without allow-listing it.** 25 total calls in 21 days is not enough for any production dialer.
- **Reach out before launch for known high-volume use cases.** Allow-listing a project takes a Support ticket, not a code change.
